## 2. Method — precise scoping

1. **Flag-definer detection from recipe lines only.** Matched `-D<flag>` for the 26
   flags but ignored matches inside comment text. This is why the System V R2/R3 RTU
   targets `rtus5`/`rtus5r3` were **kept** — their only `-DRTU` mentions were in
   advisory comments, not in the recipe (their recipes use the kept `ATTSV`/`SVR3`).

2. **Dispatch closure to a fixpoint.** Starting from the 31 flag-definers, repeatedly
   added any target whose recipe invokes (via `$(MAKE)`) a target already marked for
   deletion, until no more were found. This pulled in the 7 aliases
   (`unixware7`, `uw7`, `uw7iksd`, `uw7iksdudk`, `sinix541i`, `iclsys5r4_486`, and
   `lynx22` — the last of which a naive single pass had missed).

3. **Dangling-reference check.** Confirmed that **no kept target** references any
   removed target (zero dangling `$(MAKE)` invocations or prerequisites). Independent
   QNX targets `qnx6`/`qnx8i` do not depend on the removed `qnx_nto2+`.

4. **Stanza-accurate deletion.** Each target's full stanza — its attached descriptive
   comment block, the header, the recipe lines, and one trailing blank line — was
   removed by an exact-boundary script (no line-number drift, no orphaned comments).

5. **Platform-menu pruning.** Removed the 28 top-of-file "`# ? for … "make X"`" menu
   lines for the deleted targets. Two **mixed** entries were rewritten rather than
   deleted, to keep their still-valid alternatives:
   - 386BSD: `"make 386bsd" … "make bsd44" or "make bsd44c"` → `"make bsd44" or "make bsd44c"`.
   - PC/IX: `"make pcix" or "make is3"` → `"make is3"` (`is3`/`is3gcc` are kept).

6. **Residual comment cleanup** (`dd1cccd`): removed the two `#In case of problems, add
   back the -DRTU switch.` lines on `rtus5`/`rtus5r3`; `-DRTU` is now a no-op since the
   `RTU` source branches were removed in `SIMPLIFY_20260613_3.md`.

## 3. Deliberately KEPT

- **SVR4-only dead targets** that define none of the 26 flags: `unixware20`,
  `unixware21`, `uw20`, `uw21`, `uw213`, `uw21iksd`, `unixware`, `unixwarenetc`,
  `uw10`, `sys5r4*`, `esixr4`, `dgux430`, `rtu` (System III), `rtus5`, `rtus5r3`,
  `qnx6`, `qnx8i`, `is3`, `is3gcc`, etc. They remain valid because `SVR4`/`SVR3`/
  `ATTSV` are still defined (see below).
- **`SVR4`, `SVR3`, `ATTSV`** were **not** removed from the source or makefile: they
  are load-bearing on macOS and BSD via the `MACOSX10` → `MACOSX` → `BSD44` → `SVR4`
  → `SVR3`/`ATTSV` chain in `ckcdeb.h`. Verified post-edit that they remain defined
  under `gcc -E -DMACOSX10` and `-DBSD44`.
- All live build infrastructure: `linux`, `linuxa`, `xermit`, `wermit`, `macos`,
  `macosx`, `freebsd`, `netbsd`, `openbsd`, etc. — confirmed present after the edit.
